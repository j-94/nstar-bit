The Broader Picture

published research
"The graph was confident about things it had never actually tested."

After 7 epochs of operation, an AI knowledge graph had accumulated 0.775 confidence points per relation through declaration alone — not through evidence. When the graph was audited against actual evidence, 87 relations that were hardened at confidence ≥ 0.95 had zero supporting evidence. The same failure mode exists across decades of clinical medicine. The solution is the same: beliefs enter at confidence = 0.0 and earn their way up.

Episteme applies this primitive to institutional knowledge: every belief has a support set, every support set has an expiry condition, and when evidence is retracted or overturned, confidence decays automatically — not on a 20-year lag.
